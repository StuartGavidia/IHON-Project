Historical Context

The Lives of the 12 Caesars showcased events that supported and ultimately led to the rule of Augustus.
The author of The Lives of the 12 Caesars, Suetonius, focused on explaining why Augustus was set to be ruler of Rome because of Prophecy, by referencing numerous Roman figures like Quintus Catulus and Marcus Cicero.
Along with those sources, it is also important to keep in mind that the Romans, as described by Suetonius, are a society that truly believes in prophecy. Omens are what eventually convinced Julius Caesar to choose Augustus as his successor.
Prophecy for a future ruler plays a big role in Roman culture, especially as Rome faced political problems. Like the conspiracy of Catiline mentioned by Suetonius, and soon after, the assasination if Julius Caesar.
